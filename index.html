Welcome

I am a Heising–Simons Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of California, Santa Barbara, working in theoretical physics. My research focuses on the quantum structure of spacetime, with particular emphasis on black holes, horizonless compact objects, and gravitational waves. I study how microscopic descriptions of black holes—such as fuzzballs and extremely compact objects (ECOs)—can provide insight into the black hole information paradox and the thermodynamics of compact gravitational systems. More broadly, my work explores connections between quantum gravity and observable phenomena, with the goal of identifying potential signatures of fundamental physics in gravitational-wave data.

I completed my PhD at The Ohio State University under the supervision of Samir D. Mathur, where my research focused on black hole microstates in string theory and their low-energy gravitational descriptions. Originally from Jaipur, India, I received my integrated B.S.–M.S. degree in physics from IISER Bhopal.

Beyond research, I am interested in communicating ideas in fundamental physics to a broader audience. I run a YouTube channel, Time Before Space, where I discuss conceptual questions in gravity and quantum theory and interview physicists about their work and perspectives on foundational problems.

Madhur Mehta

Theoretical Physics · Black Holes · Quantum Gravity

I am a Heising–Simons Postdoctoral Fellow at UC Santa Barbara. My research focuses on black hole physics, the quantum structure of spacetime, and horizonless compact objects such as fuzzballs and extremely compact objects (ECOs). I study how microscopic descriptions of black holes in string theory connect to gravitational dynamics and observable phenomena.

More recently, I am developing expertise in gravitational wave physics to explore whether quantum gravity effects could leave detectable signatures in astrophysical observations. My work addresses the black hole information paradox and the thermodynamics of compact horizonless objects.
